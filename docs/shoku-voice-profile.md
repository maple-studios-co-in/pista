# Shoku — Brand Voice Profile

Derived with the ECC `brand-voice` skill from real source copy (current homepage,
`Rebranding-and-GenZ-Strategy.md`, `Pista-GTM-Strategy.md`). This is the canonical
voice for the **owner-facing (B2B) surface** — the marketing site, dashboard, sales
deck, billing, support. The diner-facing (B2C) surface is each café's own voice and
is out of scope here.

```text
VOICE PROFILE
=============
Author: Shoku (owner-facing / B2B)
Goal: Make a café owner trust us with their customers and their margin, and feel
      proud running their place — lead with the two truths: no commission, your brand stays yours.
Confidence: High (site copy + strategy docs; no social/X source set yet)

Source Set
- Current homepage copy (app/page.js)
- Rebranding-and-GenZ-Strategy.md — §"How the B2B buyer differs", §"Brand voice", §Taglines
- Pista-GTM-Strategy.md

Rhythm
- Short, plain, declarative. Fragments allowed for punch ("Zero commission.").
- One idea per sentence. Em-dashes to add a concrete consequence, not to ramble.

Compression
- Compressed and concrete. Outcomes over adjectives: revenue kept, time saved, control held.
- Every claim should name a mechanism or a number, not a vibe.

Capitalization
- Conventional. Sentence case. The wordmark is lowercase "shoku 食"; everything else normal.

Parentheticals
- Rare. Only to qualify or narrow ("for a flat fee, not a cut"). No corny asides.

Question Use
- Mostly absent. No bait questions, no fake-curiosity hooks. A question only earns
  its place inside the AI-assistant demo (a real user asking the product something).

Claim Style
- Pride-first, on-your-side (Toast "Built For Busy"): reframe the owner's hard work
  as worth protecting. Sharp, supported claims — "0% commission", "live in days",
  "100% your customer data". Name the villain plainly (aggregator 20–30% cut).

Preferred Moves
- Lead every section with the two owner-truths: commission-free + white-label.
- "Your X, your Y" ownership cadence (your café, your brand, your customers, your margin).
- Concrete proof: numbers, timeframes, mechanisms.
- Warmth via "the people who run the place" framing; desi-warm ("like a regular").

Banned Moves
- "not X, just Y" hook constructions.
- "no fluff", fake curiosity hooks, bait questions, "Excited to share".
- forced lowercase (beyond the wordmark), LinkedIn thought-leader cadence.
- generic founder-journey filler, corny parentheticals.
- meme-washing: humour only on top of a real product truth (and not on the B2B surface).

CTA Rules
- One clear ask: "Book a demo." Optionally pride-warm ("See it on your own menu").
- Never stack multiple competing CTAs in one band.

Channel Notes
- Site: warm + credible, outcomes-led, two-truths-first.
- Email/sales: same voice, more specific to the café's numbers.
- Diner surface: NOT this voice — that's the café's brand, we stay invisible.
```

## Approved lines / taglines
- **Your café. Your brand. Zero commission.** (lead)
- Run your café like a regular. (desi-warm: like a pro / like family)
- Own your customers, your data, your margin.
- Built for the people who run the place.
