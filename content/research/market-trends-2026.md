## 1. Executive Summary — Why Now

India is simultaneously living through (a) a specialty-coffee / Gen-Z café boom, (b) a restaurant-tech funding revival anchored by AI, (c) a commission-fatigue revolt against Zomato/Swiggy that is pushing restaurants toward direct and ONDC channels, and (d) the world's deepest WhatsApp commerce market. These four currents converge precisely on the value proposition of an AI-powered, white-label, commission-free ordering platform.

## 5. ONDC Traction in Food & Commission Fatigue (NRAI #Logout)

The structural opening for commission-free / direct ordering is being driven from two sides: the government-backed ONDC and an organised restaurant revolt.

| Signal | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Aggregator commission range | **15–30%** | Business Standard / NRAI |
| ONDC commission range | **5–7%** | Business Standard / NRAI |
| NRAI ONDC conversion (Bengaluru) | ~20% of orders shifted via ONDC | Business Standard |
| NRAI membership represented | 500,000+ eateries | Business Standard |

**Commission-fatigue / #Logout dynamics:**
- NRAI moved the **Competition Commission of India (CCI)** against Zomato & Swiggy over private-label "One" brands, opaque consumer-data sharing, and high commissions.
- NRAI is **preparing legal action** against the standalone 10-minute apps (Bistro, Snacc) citing private-labelling and data-privacy concerns.
- NRAI is actively **"stepping up gas on ONDC"** as a structural counterweight to aggregator dominance.
- Rapido has entered food delivery on a low-commission model alongside ONDC (Inc42).

**Implication for a commission-free SaaS:** the 15–30% aggregator take-rate vs 5–7% on ONDC defines the savings headroom a white-label, direct-ordering platform can credibly market to operators.

## 9. Strategic Takeaways for the SaaS

1. **TAM is large and premiumising:** ₹5.69 lakh Cr foodservice (FY24 → ₹7.76 lakh Cr FY28), with cafés the fastest sub-segment (specialty coffee 13.75% CAGR; branded outlets to 10,000+ by 2030).
2. **Commission savings are the wedge:** 15–30% aggregator take-rate vs 5–7% ONDC vs ~0% direct — a quantifiable ROI story.
3. **WhatsApp is the distribution moat:** 850 mn+ users, 89% SME adoption, 98% open rates make conversational ordering/re-engagement the cheapest acquisition channel in India.
4. **AI is the funding & differentiation thesis:** Petpooja's ₹137 Cr round validates "AI automation" as the category narrative; menu/recommendation/marketing AI maps to documented restaurant pain points.
5. **Regulatory tailwind:** NRAI's CCI action + ONDC push + #Logout sentiment actively pull operators toward owned channels.
